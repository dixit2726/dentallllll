Understanding Impacted Teeth

Impacted teeth are a common dental problem that can cause pain, swelling, infection, and long-term oral health issues if not treated on time. Many people are unaware of impacted teeth until they start experiencing discomfort. Understanding this condition early can help prevent complications and protect your overall dental health.

An impacted tooth is a tooth that fails to fully emerge (erupt) into its normal position in the mouth. This usually happens when there is not enough space in the jaw or when the tooth is blocked by other teeth.

Anatomy of Impaction

Types of Impaction

Not all impacted teeth are the same. Proper classification is the first step to successful treatment.

Fully Impacted

The tooth remains completely stuck under the gum and bone.

Partially Impacted

Part of the tooth breaks through the gum, creating a trap for bacteria.

Horizontal

The tooth is lying sideways, pushing directly into the roots of adjacent teeth.

Angular

The tooth is tilted at an angle, either toward or away from the neighboring tooth.

Why Does It Happen?

Impacted teeth can occur due to several factors, with the most common being a lack of space in the jaw. Evolutionarily, human jaws have become smaller, while we still retain the same number of teeth our ancestors needed for a coarser diet.

Other causes include genetics, abnormal tooth growth patterns, overcrowding, and delayed eruption of baby teeth. At Vijaya Dental, we use 3D imaging to understand the exact trajectory of your teeth before recommending any procedure.

Warning Signs

Impacted teeth often "whisper" before they "shout." Listen for these common symptoms.

Severe Pain

Dull, throbbing pain in the back of the jaw that radiates to the ear or head.

Gum Swelling

Redness, tenderness, or swelling in the gums around the impacted area.

Jaw Stiffness

Difficulty or pain when opening the mouth or chewing certain foods.

Bad Breath

A persistent unpleasant taste or odor caused by bacteria trapped under the gum flap.

The Role of Invisalign

In many cases, misaligned teeth can lead to crowding, which in turn causes other teeth to become impacted. Invisalign helps align teeth properly and reduces the risk of future impaction by creating the necessary space in the dental arch.

By using clear aligners to guide teeth into their ideal positions, we can often prevent the need for more complex surgical extractions later in life. For patients in Dammaiguda, Vijaya Dental offers advanced Invisalign treatment as part of a holistic approach to tooth alignment.

  • Better Spacing: Ensures every tooth has its place.
  • Improved Hygiene: Straighter teeth are easier to clean around.
  • Reduced Crowding: Stops teeth from blocking each other's growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do impacted teeth always need to be removed?

Not always. If the tooth is fully impacted, not causing pain, and not likely to damage adjacent teeth, we may simply monitor it. However, if symptoms arise, removal is necessary.

Is the extraction painful?

The procedure is performed under anesthesia, so you won't feel anything during the surgery. Post-op discomfort is managed with mild medications and usually fades within 48 hours.

How long does the surgery take?

A typical surgical extraction takes between 30 and 60 minutes, depending on the complexity of the impaction.

What is a 'dry socket'?

A dry socket is a painful condition that occurs if the blood clot in the extraction site is dislodged. We provide specific aftercare instructions to help you avoid this completely.

Can impacted teeth cause headaches?

Yes. The pressure from an impacted tooth can radiate through the jaw and cause tension headaches or earaches.

At what age should I check for impacted wisdom teeth?

We recommend a screening OPG X-ray around ages 16-18. Early detection allows for much simpler and safer treatment before the roots fully develop.
