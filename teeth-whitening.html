The Science of Tooth Discoloration

To understand how whitening works, we must first understand why teeth stain. Tooth discoloration is categorized into two types: Extrinsic and Intrinsic.

Extrinsic stains are on the surface of the enamel. They are typically caused by "chromogens"—pigmented molecules found in coffee, tea, red wine, and tobacco. These molecules stick to the protein pellicle that covers your teeth.

Intrinsic stains occur within the tooth's structure (the dentin). These can be caused by aging, trauma to the tooth, or exposure to certain medications (like tetracycline) during tooth development. Professional whitening is unique because it uses powerful oxygenating agents that can penetrate the enamel to break down both types of stains, something store-bought toothpastes cannot do.

Instant transformation: Our professional systems reach deep stains for a long-lasting bright result.

Why Choose Professional Whitening Over DIY Kits?

It is tempting to pick up a box of whitening strips at the pharmacy, but there are three major reasons to choose a clinical setting:

  • Concentration: Professional gels have a much higher concentration of hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide, delivering results in one hour that would take weeks at home.
  • Customization: We create a custom barrier for your gums, ensuring the whitening agent only touches your teeth and not your sensitive soft tissues.
  • Expert Supervision: We evaluate your enamel health and tooth sensitivity before starting, ensuring the procedure is 100% safe for your unique mouth.

Types of Whitening at Vijaya Dental

We offer two primary ways to brighten your smile, based on your timeline and preference:

1. In-Office Power Whitening (Laser/Zoom!)

This is our most popular option. A high-concentration gel is applied to the teeth and activated by a specialized UV or Laser light. The light accelerates the chemical reaction, breaking down stains in three 15-minute cycles. You walk out with a noticeably whiter smile in just one visit.

2. Custom Take-Home Kits

We take impressions of your teeth to create custom-fit trays. You apply professional-grade gel to these trays and wear them for 30-60 minutes a day. This is an excellent option for gradual whitening and for maintaining your results after an in-office session.

The Whitening Experience: What to Expect

The process is straightforward and comfortable at our Hyderabad clinic.

Step 1: Clinical Evaluation

We check for cavities and gum health. Whitening should only be performed on a healthy mouth. We also use a "shade guide" to determine your current tooth color and set a target for the result.

Step 2: Protection

We apply a liquid "gum dam" and retractors to keep your lips and gums safe from the whitening gel.

Step 3: Gel Application and Light Activation

The gel is applied precisely to the tooth surfaces. The laser or UV light is then positioned. During this time, you can relax, listen to music, or watch a movie.

Step 4: Post-Treatment Fluoride

After the session, we apply a desensitizing fluoride gel to strengthen the enamel and minimize any post-treatment sensitivity.

The result: A radiant, natural-looking white smile that boosts your total confidence.

Managing Sensitivity: A Safe Approach

A common concern is "zingers" or tooth sensitivity after whitening. At Vijaya Dental, we use "buffered" whitening gels that maintain a neutral pH, significantly reducing the risk of sensitivity. For patients with naturally sensitive teeth, we recommend using a desensitizing toothpaste for a week before the procedure. Our laser whitening technology is also much gentler on the nerves than older UV systems.

Maintaining Your Results: The "White Diet"

For the first 48 hours after whitening, your teeth are slightly more porous and susceptible to staining. We recommend following the "White Diet"—avoiding coffee, tea, red wine, soy sauce, and colorful fruits during this critical window. Long-term maintenance involves regular brushing and touch-up sessions every 6-12 months.

When is Whitening Not Enough?

Whitening only works on natural tooth structure. It will not change the color of existing crowns, bridges, or fillings. Additionally, some deep intrinsic stains (like those from childhood antibiotics) may not respond fully to bleaching. In these cases, we may recommend Porcelain Veneers or Composite Bonding to achieve the desired brightness.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many shades whiter will my teeth get?

Every person's teeth are unique, but on average, patients see a brightness increase of 5 to 8 shades. The result depends on the nature of the stains and your starting color.

Is professional whitening safe for enamel?

Yes! Clinical studies have shown that professional whitening, when performed correctly, does not damage or "thin" the tooth enamel. It simply removes the organic stains trapped within it.

How long do the whitening results last?

Results typically last 6 months to 2 years. This depends heavily on your diet and smoking habits. Regular touch-ups can keep your smile bright indefinitely.

Can I whiten my teeth if I have crowns?

You can whiten your natural teeth, but the crowns will stay the same color. If your crowns are now darker than your whitened teeth, they may need to be replaced for a perfect match.

Is there an age limit for teeth whitening?

We generally recommend whitening for patients 16 years and older, as the tooth's pulp (nerve) is fully developed by then, reducing the risk of sensitivity.

What is the cost of teeth whitening in Hyderabad?

In-office professional whitening is more expensive than take-home kits but offers instant results. We provide competitive pricing for both options at our Dammaiguda clinic.
